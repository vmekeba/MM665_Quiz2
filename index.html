Contradictory Class Snacks
These snacks will turn you into a social paraiah.

  • Home
  • Crunchy
  • Chewy and Sticky
  • Pungent
  • Staining
  • Spillable
  • Cellophane Wrapped
  • Multiple Pieces
  • Allergenic
  • Toxic
  • Illicit

Crunchy!

If you're craving the crunch, there are a bunch of snacks that fit the bill. Chips, pretzels, fried pork skins, celery, carrots, jicama and Wheat Thins will all make a resounding crunch during a lecture.

Chewy and Sticky!

If chewy and sticky is what you desire, gum, caramel, taffy, s'mores, Sugar Daddies and Tootsie Rolls will threaten to pull out your dental fillings and send you to the Student Health Center.

Pungent!

Although Umami is "in," eating gorganzola or camembert cheese, durian, pickled herring, sardines, kimchi, or smoked salmon during a study group might make you a study group of one.

Staining!

You might be jonesing for those Flaming Hot Dorritos, but remember that they will stain your library book pages. If your sudent budget affords you to pay your library fine, then go ahead and indulge in those Cheetos, Kool-Aid Packets, Takis and Jolly Ranchers.

Spillable!

Let's face it, we all like to dip. But snacking on salsa, fondue, french dip, sausage dip and hummus during lab can cause a hazardous mess!

Cellophane-Wrapped!

Although very portable, cellophane-wrapped snacks are not welcome in study hall. Rustling the wrappers on Moon Pies, Blow Pops, Twinkies, hard butterscotch, Starlight Mints and Ding Dongs will send you straight to detention.

Multiple Pieces!

Skittles, Milk Duds, M&Ms, Smarties, Tic Tacs and Gummi Bears are super easy to put in your backpack, but one spill will send these rainbow-colored treats cascading down the classroom aisle.

Allergenic!

Facts. That peanut butter sandwhich that you have been craving can send your TA into anaphylactic shock. Don't risk it...or getting an incomplete in your class.

Toxic!

We all love sushi. But the Blowfish Sushi Challenge is not one that you should partake in during AP Chem. Just focus on your Periodic Table of the Elements.
